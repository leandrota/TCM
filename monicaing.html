Discover More About Brazil's Most Beloved Characters

Mônica is an iconic character from Brazilian comics, created by Mauricio de Sousa. She is known for her strong personality, short hair, red dress and the inseparable rabbit plush, Samson. Mônica is courageous and fearless, not hesitating to face challenges and defend her friends. Her famous bunny kick is her signature move when she gets angry. She leads the gang Mônica, a group of friends who live many adventures and teach valuable lessons about friendship and respect. Mônica is an enduring symbol of Brazilian culture and a model of determination and courage for children of all ages.

Mônica, now 15 years old, no longer displays the short stature and corpulence of her childhood, although She still maintains her characteristic little teeth and hair that falls in locks below her ears. She is innate leader, endowed with a heart that can make mistakes, but is filled with purity and resilience, demonstrating remarkable strength and independence. Just like in her original version, Mônica continues to have a short fuse, being somewhat clumsy. and not worrying too much about traditional standards of femininity. Although occasionally tries to behave in a more "ladylike" manner, her bossy nature is counterbalanced by her kindness and natural ability to take control of situations.
